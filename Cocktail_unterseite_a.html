Caipirinha aus Brasilien

Ingredients

  • Cachaca (6 cl)
  • Limette (1 ps)
  • Cane Sugar (2-3 ts)
  • Crushed Ice

preperation

First, cut the lime into 8 pieces. Place the lime wedges into a tumbler, add the cane sugar, and extract the lime juice using a muddler. Then, fill the glass with crushed ice and add the Cachaca. Stir everything well and serve the Caipirinha with a straw.

Background

The classic Caipirinha, often referred to as Caipirinha de Pinga, consists of a special blend of Cachaça, lime juice, sugar, and ice. It is a prime example of the Sour category and offers a perfect balance between sweetness and acidity. However, the variety doesn't end there. Alternatively, there are versions with vodka, known as Caipirinha de Vodka or Caipiroska, or with red wine, mainly served in southern Brazil as Caipirinha de Vinho. Originally, the Caipirinha originates from the cocktail Batida de Limão, the classic sour without pulp.
The name "Caipirinha" is an affectionate diminutive form of the Brazilian word "Caipira," which roughly translates to "country bumpkin." Despite this humble-sounding name, the Caipirinha was initially a favorite among Brazil's rural upper class.
After World War I, it captured the hearts of urban dwellers, while in rural areas, pure sugarcane spirits continued to be enjoyed. Thus, from a simple "country bumpkin," it became an internationally celebrated cocktail that captures the vibrant culture and sunny temperament of Brazil in a glass.
